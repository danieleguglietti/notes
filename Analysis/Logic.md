***Logic*** is the language that defines the foundations of the modern *mathematics language*. 
Some important fields like **set theory**, **model theory** and **proof theory** are considered *direct* subareas of mathematical logic.
Even though it's one of the most primitive subjects, the study and development of the **logic frameworks** are begun just in the late 19th century with the start of axiomatic frameworks for *geometry*, *analysis*, *arithmetic* and others.

Nowadays the study of the *logic language* is the first step if you want to start a course based upon mathematics. 
Developing good skills in logic processes will surely help in the understanding of both easy and advanced mathematics fields.

### Propositions
The main object of logic language is the ***proposition***.
A *proposition* is a statement which can be both true or false (which we refers as *T* and *F*).

**ex.** "*Socrates's a man*";
**ex.** "*Pythagoras's eating an apple*";
**ex.** "3 is a natural number";
**ex.** "$2 + 2 \geq 5$";
